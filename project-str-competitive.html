STR Competitive Analysis:
Jakarta vs Bali

Using Smith Travel Research (STR) methodology to benchmark performance against competitive sets and identify revenue optimization opportunities.

The Challenge

Making pricing decisions without looking at what competitors are doing is risky. You might be charging too low and losing revenue, or pricing too high and losing bookings.

I analyzed two luxury properties (Jakarta and Bali) using STR STAR Report methodology, which is the standard way hotels benchmark themselves against competitors. The goal is to figure out where we're doing well, where we're falling behind, and why.

Data & Methodology

Scope: 2 years of data (2024-2025), daily granularity
Properties:

  • Jakarta: 280-room business hotel (Property_JAK_001)
  • Bali: 180-room luxury resort (Property_BAL_001)

Competitive Framework:

  • Competitive Set: 5-7 direct competitors with similar pricing/positioning
  • Market: All luxury hotels in the area (broader benchmark)
  • STR Indices: MPI (occupancy), ARI (rate), RGI (revenue)

What I Learned

Looking at just occupancy and ADR, both properties seemed okay. Jakarta was at around 60% occupancy and Bali at 80%, with stable rates. But when I compared them to competitors using the STR indices, the real issues became clear. We're losing market share.

Each property needs different fixes:

  • Jakarta needs to work on distribution and visibility
  • Bali needs to increase rates and improve positioning
